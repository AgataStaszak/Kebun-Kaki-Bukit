Our Farm

Kebun Kaki Bukit is situated in an approximately 3 acre plot of land with an acre large fresh water pond (sourced from the mountains) used for fish rearing. Our farm is being developed with the goal of becoming a sustainable demo farm for education on natural farming, aquaponics/vaquaponics and natural construction. We are using permaculture principles, effective microorganisms (EM-1), agnihotra as well as other natural farming techniques. We also have many mini satellite fruit farms producing lemon, lime and passion fruit in the vicinity which we help to manage. We are affiliated with local educational institutions such as University Putra Malaysia and oversea universities for internship programs on agriculture, environmental engineering and natural construction.
We will be rearing fish in prototype High-density polyethylene (HDPE) floating tanks and growing vegetables and herbs in rafts floating on the pond. Living quarters also use HDPE as the building material for walls, doors, windows and many other parts.
We are cultivating mangosteen trees, ciku, coconuts and cempedak. We also grow many varieties of herbs, as well as papaya, coconut, calamansi, lemon, sugarcane, pineapple and passion fruit. We are also home to chickens, guinea fowls, a pair of geese, ducks and in the future, possibly rabbits.
We started off as a dragon fruit and durian farm at the foot of Jugra Hills, and went on to Kg. Kuantan, Kuala Selangor in late 2013. We have relocated to the hills of Janda Baik as of September 2016. Located 50 km north of Kuala Lumpur, we can be found in a traditional Malay village - Kampung Janda Baik, Bentong, Pahang. We have come a long way with the help of volunteers from all over the world.

Our Team

Vickey

Director

With a passion for conservation work, a sense of love towards the nature of Malaysia, a fascination with international and local cuisines and knowledge about environmental biology gained during her studies in New South Wales, Australia, Vickey found her way to Kebun Kaki Bukit to start a brand new adventure.

David

Manager

Involved in organic farming since 2007, David has gained experience by managing distressed or abandoned farms with the help of volunteers from all over the world. After starting the Kebun Kaki Bukit project in 2013, he is now involved in R&D of farming techniques such as aquaponics, foliar boosters and natural construction.

Eugene

Sales & Marketing

This tireless and always cheerful man makes sure that no day on the farm is uneventful and that work always goes smoothly. He's learning farming techniques from David as they work together to develop the KKB farm.

Azolla
